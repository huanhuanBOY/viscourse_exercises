Visualization Design

The Securities and Futures Commission of Hong Kong has built a database to store all the news articles related to finance and stock. They want to develop a visualization system which can show the sentiments of different groups of people towards a finance policy and whether the sentiments change over time. For example, from the following articles, we know that Meng Xiaoming is negative on the first policy but is positive on second policy.

Article 1: Financial industry in Hong Kong and China slams government debt swap programme …. “The government’s manner of enforcing the debt swap has been very crude,” said Meng Xiaoning, chief executive of TF Securities in Hong Kong.

Article 2: China's watchdogs step in to avert Sinosteel bond default Mainland regulators make rare intervention in bond market, with the company's debt holders told to redeem their notes a month later … Meng Xiaoning, the chief executive of TF Securities, said he was cautiously optimistic on the drive to internationalise the country's bond market. …

(a). You are asked to design a visualization which can show the sentiment changes of a person towards a set of given topics.

Design requirements: 1. whether show multiple topics at the same time 2. whether show the temporal information 3. whether show the sentiment intuitively

(b). You are asked to extend the above visualization to compare the sentiments of two persons toward a set of given topics.

The sentiment might change over time for each of them. You need to show the sentiment similarity and difference of these two persons toward different topics.

Design requirements: Besides the requirements in (a), we need: whether the overall and detailed similarity and difference can be easily observed through the visualization.
